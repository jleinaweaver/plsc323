The process we are following this semester is informed by everything we’ve done over the last five weeks


The process acknowledges that all community problems exist in political arenas

  • e.g. places with established patterns of interests, institutions and interactions

  • Considering the “politics” of the problem is meant to help us navigate the community and maximize our chances of success!


The process acknowledges the importance of contested concepts in problem-solving

  • “Environment” is an ever-evolving and contested concept with no “correct” answer

  • BUT getting everyone to accept a common definition is incredibly powerful!

  • And that’s why we don’t actually “frame” the problem for the community until AFTER hearing from the community!
