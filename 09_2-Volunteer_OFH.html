The first step I want you to do on your own.

  • Everybody take out a piece of paper and something to write with


SLIDE: Step 1

Describing the Problem: Step 1


Summarize your assignment submission as a premise in this argument

  • e.g. Food waste is a problem caused by…

  • e.g. Food waste creates serious economic costs such as…

Working on your own I want you to take a few minutes to reflect on your assignment submission and then convert it into a premise in your group’s argument

  • Your premise should be a complete sentence that adopts this structure

  • Write the premise on the first line of the paper


Ok, below the premise I want you to describe the evidence you have brought to support this premise

  • Again, complete sentences!


Ok, next I need you to evaluate the quality of the evidence given your premise

  • Is this evidence high enough quality to convincingly establish the likelihood that your premise is correct? Why or why not?

  • Complete sentences!


SLIDE: Back to the group

The Arguments

  • Therefore, there are significant economic factors creating food waste

  • Therefore, there are serious economic consequences of food waste


The Argument Diagrams

  1. Consolidate (and order) the premises, and

  2. Specify the evidence underpinning each

Ok groups, review all the work that has been written down and work to develop your argument on the board

  • You’ll likely have premises that overlap so combine them!

  • You should order them from strongest premises to weakest

    • This is not a critique of what anyone submitted, but about building our most convincing argument!

  • Under each premise you should specify the supporting evidence


Questions on this step?

  • Go!


PRESENT and DISCUSS each

  • Present your argument to the other group making clear where evidence is lacking or uncertainty is high


What specific parts of these arguments need more or better evidence?


Are there any gaps in either argument we need to fill in?

  • e.g. types of people, species or places likely impacted but not included in our evidence?


How applicable are these arguments to our local community?

  • In other words, are these arguments too generic or general to apply to our backyard?
