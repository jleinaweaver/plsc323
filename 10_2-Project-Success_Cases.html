Remind me, what are the characteristics of a common pool resource?

  • (SLIDE)

Common Pool Resources (CPR)


Resource System

Resource Units


  1. They are large systems

    • Resource systems that are so large “as to make it costly (but not impossible) to exclude potential beneficiaries from obtaining benefits from its use.”

  2. That produce a flow of resource units

    • “…it is essential to distinguish between the resource system and the flow of resource units produced by the system, while still recognizing the dependence of one on the other” (Ostrom 1990, 30).

  3. With an aim of maximizing flow without harming the stock

    • “Resource systems are best thought of as stock variables that are capable, under favorable conditions, of producing maximum quantity of a flow variable without harming the stock or the resource system itself (Ostrom 1990, 30).”


Give me examples of how you translated your specific environmental problem into a CPR problem

SLIDE: Necessary and sufficient conditions

Qualitative Comparative Analysis (QCA)


  • A necessary condition is a factor that must be present for an outcome to occur (e.g. a requirement)

  • A sufficient condition is a factor that guarantees an outcome (e.g. a trigger)

Has anybody done any QCA work or other qualitative work with necessary and sufficient conditions?


Examples of necessary conditions:

  • Being a citizen to vote: You must be a citizen to vote in the US, but not all citizens vote.

  • Having wheels for a car: A car must have wheels, but wheels alone do not make a car.


Examples of sufficient conditions:

  • Winning the lottery for being rich: If you win, you are rich, but you could become rich other ways.

  • Rain for wet ground: If it rains, the ground is guaranteed to be wet, but it could be wet from a sprinkler.


Make sense?
